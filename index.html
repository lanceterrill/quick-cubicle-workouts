🏃 Moderate physical activity reduces your risk of disease, strengthens bones and muscles, and supports cardiovascular health. Plus, exercise boosts energy, mood, and focus — making your workday more positive and productive. Feeling shy? Find an empty conference room, or better yet, invite a colleague! 🙌
01 / 10
One-Legged Hamstring Stretch 🦵
Targets: Hamstrings · Lower Back

Put one foot up on your desk, bend at the hips, and reach toward your toes. Keep your back flat and avoid rounding your spine for a deeper, safer stretch.

💡 Tight hamstrings can worsen lower back pain — especially after long hours of sitting.
02 / 10
Seated Chair Crunches 🪑
Targets: Core · Hip Flexors

Scoot toward the front of your seat and grip the armrests for stability. Crunch your knees toward your chest while staying upright, then extend your legs straight out. Lower your legs further for more of a challenge.

💡 Keep your chest up — resist the urge to hunch forward.
03 / 10
Alternating Toe Touches 🤸
Targets: Core · Balance · Hip Flexors

Stand up tall and lift one leg while reaching toward your toes with the opposite arm. Alternate sides fluidly and keep your chest up throughout the movement.

💡 This gets you out of your seat and fires up your core. It's a great midday energy booster! ⚡
04 / 10
Seated Oblique Crunches 💪
Targets: Obliques · Core

Sit in your chair with hands behind your head. Tilt your body to the side, angling your elbow toward your hip bone, then alternate sides. Imagine walls in front and behind you to keep from twisting forward or backward.

💡 Slow and controlled beats fast and sloppy — feel each squeeze!
05 / 10
Seated Torso Twist 🔄
Targets: Spine · Back · Core

Sit up tall and keep your hips facing forward while twisting your upper body to look over your shoulder in the direction you're turning. This stretch relieves tension and takes pressure off your spine.

💡 Hold each twist for 2–3 seconds. Great for undoing hours of hunching! 🙏
06 / 10
Desk Push-Ups 🏋️
Targets: Chest · Triceps · Core

Plant your hands on the desk just outside shoulder width, tighten your core, and lower your body until your chest is at elbow height. Avoid bending at the hips or letting them sag — maintain a straight line from head to heels.

💡 Too easy? Try wall push-ups for a lighter variation, or elevate your feet for a harder one.
07 / 10
Chair Hamstring Curls 🛝
Targets: Glutes · Hamstrings

Lie on the floor with your heels resting on your chair seat. Lift your hips off the ground, then pull your heels in toward your glutes and straighten them again — keeping your hips high throughout.

💡 Modification: Simply plant feet on the floor and lift your hips up and down (glute bridge).
08 / 10
Desk Dips 💪
Targets: Triceps · Shoulders

Place your hands on the edge of your desk, then lower your body with bent arms until they reach a 90-degree angle. Push back up and repeat. Your desk becomes the perfect arm-toning station!

💡 Keep your back close to the desk and elbows pointing backward, not out to the sides.
09 / 10
Squats 🏃
Targets: Glutes · Hamstrings · Quads

Stand up, bend your knees as if sitting in a chair, keeping your core tight and knees aligned behind your toes. Do multiple reps to get the heart pumping!

💡 Modification: Use your actual chair — stand up and sit back down with arms extended forward and core engaged. 🪑
10 / 10
Lat Pulldown 🦅
Targets: Lats · Rhomboids · Posture Muscles

From a seated position, start with arms extended overhead and pull your elbows down toward your sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together. This counters your hunched posture and strengthens your back muscles — all while sitting!

💡 Do this every hour to reset your posture. Your back will thank you! 🙌
💚 Pro tip: Set a recurring reminder every 60–90 minutes to take a short movement break. Even 5 minutes makes a real difference over the course of a workday.
